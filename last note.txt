Now the honest Playbook assessment:                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  ---                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 The Playbook — Should It Stay?                                                                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      What it was supposed to be                                                                                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  The original 3-layer CTML system had Google Docs as the permanent "brain." The Playbook was the in-app version of that — a place for meta-level learnings separate from ideas. Not "build OnTime App" (that's a Vault idea), but  
  "I do my best thinking in the first 90 minutes of the day" or "Prompt fluency = the key skill right now." The seed entries make this clear — they're system decisions, not tasks.                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  The distinction is valid in principle. Ideas are future-oriented (what to do). Playbook entries are learnings to carry forward (how to work). Both matter in a productivity system.                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  Why it doesn't feel useful right now                                                                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  It's completely disconnected from the daily loop. The loop is:                                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  Morning Boot → Capture → Work → Evening Wrap                                                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  The Playbook is never invoked at any of these points. You have to remember to go there manually. Nothing in the morning asks "here's an insight from your playbook." Nothing in the evening says "any learnings to add?" It's an  
  island.                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  It's write-only. No edit, no delete. If you write something that becomes outdated or wrong, it's stuck. That makes people hesitant to use it for anything serious.

  It competes with the Vault. The Vault already has a "System" category. Right now CTML Pipeline lives in the Vault as category System. The line between "a system idea" and "a playbook insight" is blurry.

  The honest verdict

  The concept is right. The implementation earns nothing. If you went to the Playbook screen today and added 5 entries, none of them would ever surface again unless you manually navigated back. That's a note-taking app, not a
  habit system.

  Two real options

  Option A — Remove it. Delete the Playbook nav tab and screen. Keep the db.playbook table and state.playbook so existing entries aren't lost. The app gets simpler and nothing that's actually working gets removed. If you want to   log insights, the Vault with category "System" handles it.

  Option B — Wire it into the loop (the right fix). Two small changes make it earn its place:

  1. Evening Wrap gets an "Insight to log?" field. One optional line below the reflection textarea. If filled when closing the day, it auto-adds to the Playbook. This makes adding insights a natural part of the closing ritual —
  no separate navigation needed.
  2. Morning Boot surfaces 1–2 recent Playbook entries as context above the focus input. You start the day reminded of your own principles.

  With those two changes, the Playbook becomes the connective tissue between days — your principles informing the morning, your learnings captured in the evening. Without them it's just a place things go to be forgotten.

  ---
  My recommendation: Option B, but only if you'll actually write insights. If you think you'd realistically type one sentence of learning after closing the day, wire it in — it'll be the highest-signal part of the app. If that
  feels like friction you'll skip, go with Option A and simplify. The daily loop works fine without it.
