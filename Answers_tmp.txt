  Dimension 1: The Soul (The "Why")

  These questions define the psychological motivation behind the app.
  - Identity: Is this a "Digital Brain" (storing everything so you don't have to) or a "Daily Coach"
  (driving you to complete tasks)?

A:both. I'd like to store ideas that pop up in my brain during the day, but get lost immediately as I didn't noted them down. Amd a daily coach to drive me, inspire, and motivate to reach a more worked out state of this ideas.

  - Success Metric: When you close the app at the end of the day, what makes you feel like the app
  "worked"? Is it because you did more work, or because you felt less anxious?

A: it is because I feel that I did something to move my own ideas or plans forward outside of the 9-5job.


  - The User Persona: Are you building this for a "High-Performance Professional" (speed and
  execution) or a "Creative Thinker" (capture and reflection)?

A: More a Creative thinker

  Dimension 2: The Ritual (The "How")

  These questions focus on the logic of the four daily phases.
  - Morning Boot: Should this be a rigid checklist, or a soft landing for your thoughts? Does it need
  to interact with yesterday's failures/successes?

A: this is more or less a "set a rout for today". no strickt guidence, just to know what should be a focus for that day. For example, when I have a few minutes free in the job and don't know what to do in that time, I could pop up the CTML app and ree what  I thought was a good idea to work towards.

  - Capture Phase: How much friction is too much? Is "Quick Capture" just text, or should it include
  metadata (tags, urgency)?

A: just a quick capture

  - Work Phase: Is the "Work on It" phase about tracking time spent, or about updating the state of
  progress (e.g., changing a task from 'waiting' to 'doing')?

A: more or less it should reflect on the morning idea of what was the route of the day, and then capture any actual new ideas or progress that  was made towards compleating that idea.

  - Evening Wrap: Is this a moment of closure (locking the day) or a moment of preparation (setting up
   tomorrow)?

A: This should be a conclusion of the day, a last point of summary of the day, that locks the app entirely from further interacting with it to get the feeling that "I'm done for the day" but in a satisfying way, not like a chore being completed.
